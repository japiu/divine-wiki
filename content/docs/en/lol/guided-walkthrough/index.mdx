Want to make a custom skin for League of Legends? Good news. It's not as hard as it looks.

Watch the 90-second overview, then pick a question below.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7JMbzqsJxzE

<Callout type="info" title="Custom skins are safe outside Korea and China">
  Custom skins are safe on every region except **Korea** and **China**. No bans
  since 2014 when you use trusted tools. Never use custom skins on Korean or
  Chinese servers. The anti-cheat there blocks all mods and bans accounts.
</Callout>

## Frequently asked questions

<details>
<summary>Is making a custom skin hard?</summary>

Not really. The hardest part is getting used to the tools: Maya, Photoshop, and our custom skin tools (Flint, Jade, Quartz, LtMAO).

Once you've opened them a few times, it gets easier.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What tools do I need?</summary>

You'll work with a mix of 3D, image, and code tools:

- **Maya** or **Blender** for 3D models and rigging.
- **Photoshop** (or GIMP) for textures.
- **Jade** (or Ritobin) for editing particle and bin files.
- **Our custom skin tools**: Flint, Jade, Quartz, and LtMAO.

See the [Tools page](/docs/lol/tools) for the full list and install links.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How long does it take to learn?</summary>

It depends on your experience and how fast you pick things up.

- If you already know Photoshop, you've got a head start.
- If you learn slower, that's fine. Take your time.

With a little practice each day, you can build a decent skin in about **two weeks**.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Where do I start?</summary>

Three steps:

1. Install the tools from the [Tools page](/docs/lol/tools).
2. Open the [Full walkthrough](/docs/lol/guided-walkthrough/walkthrough) and work through it from start to finish.
3. By the end, you'll have a simple skin ready to test.

Stuck on setup? Jump into our [Discord](https://discord.gg/divineskins) and ask in **#modding-help**.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What should I learn after my first skin?</summary>

Dig into the rest of the wiki for bigger techniques like better meshes, textures, visual effects, and sound effects:

- [Maya](/docs/lol/maya) for rigging and meshes.
- [Blender](/docs/lol/blender) for the free 3D alternative.
- [VFX & bins](/docs/lol/vfx-bins) for particles and visual effects.
- [Animations](/docs/lol/animations) for idle loops and custom recalls.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How do I publish my skin?</summary>

When your skin is ready to share:

1. Go to [divineskins.gg](https://divineskins.gg).
2. Log in.
3. Click **Artist Panel**.
4. Click **Create Mod**.
5. Fill in the form and upload your files.
6. Submit for review.

Our moderation team reviews every submission. Once it's approved, your skin goes live on the site.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is repathing, and do I really need it?</summary>

Repathing prevents skins from breaking each other, and it optimizes mods and load times.

It's also **required to upload to divineskins.gg**. Submissions without repathing won't pass review.

The repath step is covered in the [Full walkthrough](/docs/lol/guided-walkthrough/walkthrough). Watch that part carefully before you upload.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How do I get the skin creator role?</summary>

Once your first skin is approved, request the **skin creator** role in our [Discord](https://discord.gg/divineskins). Welcome to the family, and good luck on your first custom skin.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Where do I get help?</summary>

Our [Discord](https://discord.gg/divineskins) is the fastest way.

- **#modding-help** for tool setup and step-by-step questions.
- Ask a question any time. The community answers most posts within a few hours.

</details>
