# Recoloring VFX / Particles with Hacksaw

This tutorial covers how to recolor League of Legends VFX (particles) using **Hacksaw**, a tool built for the custom skin modding community by Martynas. You'll learn how to open `.bin` files, adjust colors, and safely save particle changes.

---

</ToolCard>

<ToolCard name="RitoBin" href="/docs/lol/tools/Ritobin">
  A converter made by Morilli to convert `.bin` to `.py`.
</ToolCard>

## Step 1: Set Up Hacksaw

1. Download and open Hacksaw
   <img src="/wiki-images/1767146058191-p47ap.png" alt="Image" width={500} />
2. Go to the **Settings** tab
   <img src="/wiki-images/1767146063211-rh626f.png" alt="Image" width={400} />
3. Click on **Select Ritobin_Cli**
   <img src="/wiki-images/1767146098137-0kfjp.png" alt="Image" width={500} />

<img src="/wiki-images/1767146109516-brjqcp.png" alt="Image" width={500} />

> 💡 Hacksaw requires `ritobin_cli` to read and write `.bin` files used for visual effects.

## Step 2: Locate the BIN File

Your particle `.bin` file will usually be located in one of the following folders:

/yourWadFolder/data/ /yourWadFolder/data/characters/YourChamp/skins/SkinXX/

`SkinXX` corresponds to your skin's ID.

<img src="/wiki-images/1767146144586-3z25vn.png" alt="Image" width={600} />

<img src="/wiki-images/1767146157874-buswuj.png" alt="Image" width={600} />

You can identify your skin number by checking the HUD folder — it typically includes preview images.

<img src="/wiki-images/1767146166101-ajvkvb.png" alt="Image" width={600} />

## Step 3: Open the BIN File

1. Go to the **Splash** tab in Hacksaw
   <img src="/wiki-images/1767146179052-z4hk9c.png" alt="Image" width={300} />
2. Click **Open Bin**
   <img src="/wiki-images/1767146185172-7lewvd.png" alt="Image" width={300} />

<img src="/wiki-images/1767146198483-5k0ae.png" alt="Image" width={500} />

You'll now see a list of particle entries like:

- `_Q_mis` → Missile VFX for Q ability
- `_W_tar` → Target hit effect for W
- `_E_cas` → Cast animation for E

> 💡 **Common Naming Terms:** mis = missile • tar = target • cas = cast • trail = trail • exp = explosion

## Step 4: Recolor the Particles

1. Pick your new color using the color palette
   <img src="/wiki-images/1767146233766-gxzbj.png" alt="Image" width={500} />
2. Choose the ParticleSystem you want to be recolored
   <img src="/wiki-images/1767146238999-n11n14.png" alt="Image" width={500} />
3. Press **Recolor Selected** on the bottom of Hacksaw
   <img src="/wiki-images/1767146242759-dx73al.png" alt="Image" width={500} />
4. Save your changes by clicking **Save Bin**
   <img src="/wiki-images/1767146246511-68brzq.png" alt="Image" width={500} />

## Step 5: Some Particles Use Textures for Coloring

Not all particles are recolored through Hacksaw's built-in color palette. Some VFX effects get their color from an external texture file — usually a `.dds` or `.tex` located in the Particles folder.

### What to Do

1. Locate the Particles folder: `Assets/Characters/LeeSin/Skins/Skin31/Particles`
2. Open the texture file in Photoshop, GIMP, or any image editing software that supports `.dds`
3. Recolor the texture manually
4. Save the edited texture as `.dds` using **BC3 / DXT5** compression
5. Replace the original file in your Particles or VFX folder

> 💡 **Common texture paths:**
>
> - `Assets/Characters/LeeSin/Skins/Skin31/Particles`
> - `Assets/Shared/Particles`
> - `Assets/Characters/LeeSin/Skins/Base/Particles`

## Important Tips

### Do Not Recolor Black or White (by Default)

Black and white values are often used for **transparency** in League VFX. Recoloring them may break or hide effects completely.

If you intentionally want to recolor black/white:

1. Go to **Settings**
2. Disable **"Ignore Black/White Values"**

---

### Understanding BlendModes

Each particle uses a BlendMode, shown on the right in Hacksaw:

| BlendMode | Behavior                                                                                |
| --------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1         | No transparency, feel free to recolor black/white                                       |
| 3         | SCB transparency                                                                        |
| 4         | Standard transparency, don't recolor too black or else it will become invisible in-game |

<img src="/wiki-images/1767146259378-b1tf5.png" alt="Image" width={500} />

> 💡 Knowing how blend modes affect rendering helps avoid visual bugs or unintended glows.
